Welcome to my corner of the internet.

I've been meaning to set up a proper personal site for a while — a place that's actually mine,
not a LinkedIn profile or a Twitter bio. This is it.

## Who I am

I'm a software engineer based in Indonesia. I work on backend systems, mostly in the distributed
systems space. But that's not all I am.

I'm also a husband and a dad, which means a significant portion of my brain is occupied at any
given moment by things like weekend plans, what's for dinner, and whether we can sneak in a short
trip before the school holiday ends. I wouldn't trade any of it.

## What I'll write about

Roughly three things:

- **Software** — distributed systems, system design, developer tooling. The technical stuff I
  think about at work and can't stop thinking about after hours.
- **Food** — I eat seriously. I believe you can learn more about a place from one good meal than
  from a full day of sightseeing. I'll write about places worth eating at and dishes worth seeking out.
- **Travel** — we travel as a family when we can. It's chaotic and slower than solo travel and
  I love it. I'll write about the places we go and what made them worth the packing.

No fixed schedule, no strict niche. Just things I find worth writing down.

## Why Astro?

I wanted something fast and simple that would let me write in MDX without fighting a framework.
Astro's content collections give me schema validation on my frontmatter, which means I get a
build error instead of a runtime surprise if I forget a field. Good enough reason for me.
